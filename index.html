What is that?

An aurora (plural: auroras or aurorae),[a] sometimes referred to as polar lights (aurora polaris), northern lights (aurora borealis), or southern lights (aurora australis), is a natural light display in the Earth's sky, predominantly seen in high-latitude regions (around the Arctic and Antarctic).

Aurora boreal expeditions!

Tromsø is one of the best places on earth to observe the Northern Lights. The most favorable months to observe this phenomenon are December, January, February and March. Our tour is an invitation to discover the beautiful small town of Tromsø, its famous Arctic cathedral, its fjords and above all to meet this fascinating natural phenomenon, the Northern lights.
